Evidence-Based Career Theories

Integrating proven career development frameworks

Our counseling method integrates proven career development theories such as Holland's Theory of Career Choice and Super's Life-Span, Life-Space Approach, both of which focus on aligning personal characteristics with suitable career paths.

We also use Krumboltz's Social Learning Theory, which emphasizes the role of learning experiences in shaping career decisions. This comprehensive methodology ensures that students are not only prepared for the future but also empowered to make career decisions based on their experiences, skills, and values.

Key Theories We Apply:
Holland's Theory: Matching personality types with work environments
Super's Life-Span Approach: Career development as a lifelong process
Krumboltz's Social Learning Theory: Learning experiences shape career decisions
